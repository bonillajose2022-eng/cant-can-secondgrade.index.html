Grammar: CAN / CAN'T

Learn the rules step by step! (¡Aprende las reglas paso a paso!)

💡 What does CAN mean?

1. Ability (Habilidad)

We use CAN to say someone is able to do something.

  • I can swim.Yo sé nadar.
  • She can play the piano.Ella sabe tocar el piano.
  • Birds can fly.Los pájaros pueden volar.

2. Permission (Permiso)

We use CAN to ask for or give permission.

  • Can I go to the bathroom?¿Puedo ir al baño?
  • You can go now.Puedes irte ahora.

3. Possibility (Posibilidad simple)

We use CAN to talk about simple possibilities.

  • It can rain today.Puede llover hoy.
  • Anyone can make mistakes.Cualquiera puede cometer errores.
🚫 What does CAN'T mean?

CAN'T = No ability / Not allowed / Not possible

  • I can't fly.No puedo volar. (No tengo esa habilidad)
  • You can't run in the classroom.No puedes correr en el aula. (No está permitido)
  • It can't snow in summer here.No puede nevar aquí en verano.
📝 Note: can't is short for cannot. Both are correct!
Nota: "can't" es la forma corta de "cannot". ¡Las dos son correctas!
🔊 can't has a clear /t/ sound!
🏗️ Sentence Structure

✅ Affirmative (Afirmativa)

Subject + CAN + base verb
  • She can dance.Ella puede bailar.
  • They can play football.Ellos pueden jugar al fútbol.
  • I can help you.Yo puedo ayudarte.

❌ Negative (Negativa)

Subject + CAN'T / CANNOT + base verb
  • He can't fly.Él no puede volar.
  • I cannot run fast.No puedo correr rápido.
  • We can't swim here.No podemos nadar aquí.

❓ Interrogative (Interrogativa)

CAN + subject + base verb + ?
  • Can you help me?¿Puedes ayudarme?
  • Can he ride a bike?¿Él sabe montar bicicleta?
  • Can they speak English?¿Ellos pueden hablar inglés?

⚡ Short Answers

In English, we usually give short answers instead of repeating the whole sentence. (En inglés usamos respuestas cortas.)

QuestionYes ✅No ❌
Can I …?Yes, you can.No, you can't.
Can you …?Yes, I can.No, I can't.
Can he …?Yes, he can.No, he can't.
Can she …?Yes, she can.No, she can't.
Can it …?Yes, it can.No, it can't.
Can we …?Yes, we can.No, we can't.
Can they …?Yes, they can.No, they can't.
💡 Remember: / Recuerda:
  • When someone asks "Can you…?", answer with "I can" / "I can't"
  • When someone asks "Can I…?", answer with "you can" / "you can't"
  • Always use the correct pronoun! (¡Usa siempre el pronombre correcto!)
📌 Key Grammar Rules

Rule 1: Always use BASE verb after CAN/CAN'T

After can or can't, NEVER add -ing, -s, -ed, or "to".

❌ "She can plays"
WRONG / MAL
✅ "She can play"
CORRECT / BIEN

Rule 2: CAN does NOT change with he/she/it

No -s, no changes! (¡Sin -s, sin cambios!)

❌ "She cans sing"
✅ "She can sing"
SubjectModalExample
I / You / He / She / It / We / Theycan ✅He can swim.
He / She / Itcans ❌He cans swim. ❌

Rule 3: CAN'T vs DON'T/DOESN'T

MeaningUseExample
Not able to (no ability)can'tI can't fly.
Not something you do (habit)don't/doesn'tI don't eat fish.
Not allowed (strong)can'tYou can't run here.

🔊 Pronunciation Notes (Para el docente)

  • "can" in a sentence → weak form: /kən/ (sounds like "kun")
    In "I can swim", "can" is weak and unstressed.
  • "CAN" emphatic → strong form: /kæn/
    In "Yes, I CAN!", "can" is stressed and clear.
  • "can't" → always clear /t/ at the end
    🔊 /kɑːnt/ — the /t/ is important!

🔧 Common Errors — Error Correction

These are mistakes students often make. Study them carefully! (Errores que los estudiantes cometen con frecuencia.)

❌ "She can to play"
Don't use "to" after can!
→
✅ "She can play"
❌ "He cans swim"
No -s with he/she/it!
→
✅ "He can swim"
❌ "Can you to help me?"
No infinitive after can!
→
✅ "Can you help me?"
❌ "I don't can"
Use can't, not don't can!
→
✅ "I can't"
❌ "She can dancing"
Use base verb, not -ing!
→
✅ "She can dance"
❌ "Can swims he?"
Use base verb in questions!
→
✅ "Can he swim?"
